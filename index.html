Where is Egypt?

Egypt is located in North Africa, adjacent to the Mediterranean Sea and the Red Sea, and is the junction of Asia and Africa. The Nile, the longest river in the world, passes through Egypt and brings water and fertile soil to it. The Nile Delta is the most densely populated area in Egypt.

The Suez Canal in Egypt connects the Mediterranean Sea and the Indian Ocean, which is an important hub for modern shipping.

Old Kingdom Period

The Old Kingdom is the period spanning 2686-2181 BC. It is the first golden age in the history of ancient Egypt. The famous pyramids at Giza -- Khufu, Khafre and Menkaure Pyramids, are built in this age. That is why the Old Kingdom is also known as the "Age of Pyramids".

New Kingdom Period

After the Old Kingdom,  ancient Egypt experienced two intermediate periods and a Middle Kingdom. In 1550 BC, Egypt started the New Kingdom period. The 18th Dynasty is the era in which ancient Egypt reached the peak of its power, during which some of the most famous Pharaohs in egypt history appeared, including Thutmose III, Amenhotep III, Akhenaten and Tutankhamun. The famous Tutankhamun golden mask was made during this period.

Do you know?

Pharaoh Amenhotep IV, who changed his name to Akenaten in honour of Aten, could be the first person who tried to carry out monotheistic religious reform, although the reform failed after his death.

 Ptolematic Kingdom Period

In 332 BC, Alexander the Great invaded Egypt. After the death of Alexander, Ptolomy, one of his closest companions, was appointed to rule Egypt, and here started Ptolematic Kingdom Period.

Ptolematic Egypt was a Hellenistic kingdom based in ancient Egypt, and the pharaoh was no longer native Egyptian. During this period, the culture from ancient Greece and Roman Empire were merged into Egypt native culture, which created a multicultural Egypt Empire.



Do you know?
